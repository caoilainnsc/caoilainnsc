About Me

Caoilainn Christensen is a dedicated Cognitive Science and Computer Science student at Pomona College with a passion for combining technology and human-centered design to create intuitive and impactful user experiences. With a strong foundation in cognitive science, computational linguistics, and software engineering, she is committed to applying her skills to make a meaningful difference through technology. Caoilainn is coursework includes Calculus I-III, Linear Algebra, Cognitive Science, Phonology, and Syntactic Analysis, which have honed her analytical and problem-solving abilities. She is also excited to expand her expertise in the future with advanced courses such as Data Structures and Advanced Programming and Introduction to Languages and the Theory of Computation. Her hands-on experience includes an internship with Old Dominion University’s Research Experience for Undergraduates, where she conducted cluster analysis on natural language data to explore neurodivergence in the criminal justice system. She is currently working with Dr. Galia Bar-Sever at Pomona College on research analyzing the evolution and spread of slang on social media platforms. Through these projects, she has developed strong programming skills in Python, Java, and data visualization, as well as a deep understanding of how people interact with digital communication platforms. With a keen interest in UI/UX design, front-end development, and computational analysis, Caoilainn is eager to contribute her skills in software engineering roles that challenge her to design thoughtful, user-centered interfaces. She is driven by a commitment to improving how people interact with technology and is excited about the possibilities for future innovation.
